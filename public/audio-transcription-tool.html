What this tool helps you do

If you are trying to figure out a melody, bass movement, guitar phrase, horn line, or piano voicing, the main problem is usually not playback itself. The problem is hearing the same short fragment enough times, navigating accurately, and confirming pitch movement with enough visual feedback.

Loop short phrases

Set A/B loop points and replay difficult passages until the line becomes clear.

Slow playback down

Reduce speed for hard runs, fast articulations, and dense rhythmic figures.

Inspect the waveform

Zoom in for more precise timing, note starts, and phrase boundaries.

Focus with EQ

Use instrument-oriented EQ presets to hear bass, guitar, piano, vocal, or drums more clearly.

Why use a local-first audio transcription tool

  • Your files stay on your device instead of being uploaded.
  • You can work quickly with local recordings, screen captures, or downloaded clips.
  • The workflow stays focused on transcription rather than full music production.
  • You get a practical note-view aid for short phrases directly in the browser.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use this with local MP3, WAV, MP4, or MOV files?

Yes. The tool is designed for local video and audio files, so it fits common practice workflows built around clips, captures, and downloaded references.

Is this a full speech-to-text transcription product?

No. This tool is aimed at music and close listening workflows: slowing playback, looping, EQ focus, waveform inspection, and note-oriented transcription support.

Does it replace musical judgment?

No. It helps you inspect and confirm what you hear more efficiently, but the final transcription still depends on your ear and interpretation.
